Retail

Six years of working in retail has taught me all kinds of lessons. For one semester, I was working 3 retail positions at once. Some of my favorites are patience, the power of being nice to people, how much smoother things are when you clean up after yourself.


Patience
When to slow down

People are awesome. There are so many ways that we as humans communicate what we want to communicate, whether it be intentional or not. Having patience to listen to people and not trying to fit people into a box (i.e. stating objectively "Nice people do [action], and they definitely don't so [other action]") is one of my favorite lessons from retail. You just never know how awesome a person is if you don't pay attention.


Being Nice
Everyone wins.

Closely tied with patience is how incredibly people's demeanor changes when you're nice to them -- especially when they don't expect you to be. I loved when this would happen. I would always take the "bad" customer. It is entirely worth the "loss of pride" to allow someone to be rude or upset, respect their emotions, and still treat them with kindness or respect. Happy feelings all around.

Clean Up After Yourself
It's worth the extra minutes

Something that has not come naturally to me (because of course you should be nice to people, but really do I have to put the labelmaker away right now?) is the power of cleaning up your space. The concept behind this is respecting people's time and energy (yes, do put the labelmaker away because then when someone needs it next, they know where to look). When you clean up after yourself, you're taking the weight off another person to have to deal with your mess. Just reducing the uncertainly alone (the hesitancy of wondering if the labelmaker will be in it's labelmaker house) encourages good habits ("I know the labelmaker will be there so I go and get it and I will use it!").

It's the same everywhere I go. When you respect people, it needs to show to reap the benefits. There are many ways of respecting people. I've learned that cleaning up after yourself has been a wonderful, tactile expression of me respecting other people.
